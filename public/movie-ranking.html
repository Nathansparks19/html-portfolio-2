The Best Movies According to Nathan Sparks

My top 3 movies of all time

Avengers Endgame

Avengers: Endgame (2019) is an epic conclusion to the Infinity Saga in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Directed by Anthony and Joe Russo, the film picks up after Infinity War, where Thanos has wiped out half of all life. The remaining Avengers, devastated and desperate, embark on a time-travel mission to retrieve the Infinity Stones and reverse the catastrophe.

The movie delivers emotional depth, stunning action sequences, and satisfying character arcs. Tony Stark’s sacrifice, Steve Rogers’ farewell, and the climactic final battle make it a thrilling and heartfelt experience. While it relies on fan service and nostalgia, Endgame successfully wraps up a decade of storytelling, making it one of the most memorable superhero films of all time.

Mulan

Mulan is a 1998 animated Disney film based on the Chinese legend of Hua Mulan. The story follows a young woman, Mulan, who disguises herself as a man to take her ailing father’s place in the army, defying societal norms. With the help of her witty dragon companion, Mushu, she trains as a soldier and ultimately plays a crucial role in saving China from the invading Huns.

The film is praised for its strong female protagonist, stunning animation, and memorable songs like Reflection and I'll Make a Man Out of You. It balances action, humor, and heartfelt moments, making it an inspiring tale of bravery, identity, and self-discovery.

The song "I'll Make a Man Out of You" really touched me, and specific lyrics in the song resonated with me, such as...
You must be swift as the coursing river
(Be a man) with all the force of a great typhoon
(Be a man) with all the strength of a raging fire
Mysterious as the dark side of the moon

Moon Knight

Moon Knight is a Marvel superhero series that stands out for its psychological depth and unique storytelling. The show follows Marc Spector, a former mercenary with Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID), who becomes the avatar of the Egyptian moon god, Khonshu. As he struggles with multiple identities, including the timid Steven Grant, he is drawn into a dark, supernatural conflict.

The series is praised for Oscar Isaac’s stellar performance, bringing complexity to Marc and Steven’s duality. Its blend of action, mythology, and psychological thriller elements makes it different from traditional Marvel shows. The darker tone, mind-bending sequences, and well-executed character development make Moon Knight a gripping watch, though some found the pacing inconsistent.

Overall, it’s a fresh and intriguing addition to the MCU, especially for fans of psychological dramas with a supernatural twist.
